

   Principle of Working of a Transistor (Excess Carrier Injection) 
A BJT works based on minority carrier injection and control of charge carriers across two p-
n junctions. 
Consider an NPN transistor: 
• The Emitter (N) is heavily doped. 
• The Base (P) is very thin and lightly doped. 
• The Collector (N) is moderately doped. 
Working Principle: 
1. Forward bias the Emitter-Base (EB) junction 
o Majority carriers (electrons in NPN) are injected from the emitter into the 
base. 
o These injected electrons become excess minority carriers in the base region. 
2. Diffusion across Base 
o Since the base is very thin and lightly doped: 
▪ Only a small number recombine with holes. 
▪ Most electrons diffuse across the base. 
3. Reverse bias the Collector-Base (CB) junction 
o The electric field in the CB junction sweeps the electrons into the collector. 
o Thus, a large collector current flows. 
Key Idea: 
A small base current controls a large collector current because: 
• The base only replaces the few recombined carriers. 
• Most injected carriers reach the collector.
